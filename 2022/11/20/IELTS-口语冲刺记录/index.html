IELTS 口语一日冲刺记录 Part 1

Part 1

Part 1 - Strategies and skills

Common triggers or questions

The 6 most common triggers or questions in IELTS Speaking Part 1 are:

  • Do you VERB?
    • How long you have done it?

      • I’ve played since I was a child.

    • How often you do it?

      • regularly, once in a blue moon, every now and again, on a fairl y regular basis

      • on a daily / weekly /monthly basis

      • rarely / hardly ever

  • Are you good at it?
    • Abilities

      • I have a bit of a knack for playing the guitar

      • I wouldn’t say I’m gifted but I can play fairly well

      • I can hold my own

      • I am pretty good at it

  • Did you VERB as a child?
    • Talk about past habits

      • I used to play when…

      • I would play in my free time

      • I often play at home

  • Do you prefer A or B?
    • Preference, compare and evaluate
    • Say which

      • I much prefer A to B, I tend to like A

    • Say why

      • A is far better than B because…

      • A is easier to use than B

  • Do you think NOUN is important?
    • Opinion, compare and evaluate
    • Give an opinion;

      • Absolutely, without a doubt, for sure

    • Say why;

      • We can learn so much from our family

    • Give an example;
  • Which NOUN is popular in your country?
    • Opinion, compare and evaluate
    • Give an opinion;

      • I reckon, I’d say, as far as I can see…

    • Say why;

      • want to be famous, make a lot of money

    • Give an example;

      • when I was in school, all of my friends wanted to be

Super strategies and top tips

  1. Short but expand
  2. Answer directly + why + example
  3. Talk about yourself
    1. Talk about more general ideas in Part 3!
  4. Be natural …with a new friend
  5. Know common question types

Learn to activate vocabulary

  1. Learn words in context
  2. Learn from sounds

  • Do you work or are you a student?

    • I work for a bank / a school / the government

    • I’ve worked there for fifteen years or so.

    • Basically my job involves designing products

      • Basically my job involves selling products
        Basically my job involves teaching others

  • What do you like about your work?

    • The best part about my job is the work environment.
      The best part about my job is my collegues.
      The best part about my job is the salary.

    • I also love the fact that we have flexitime
      I also love the fact that I am not on a 9 to 5 schedule
      I also love the fact that we get lots of training.

    • What’s more, I get to (can) work from home sometimes.
      What’s more, I get to meet some interesting people
      What’s more, I get to work with some great people

Part 1 - Model Answers

Accessories: Watches

Vocabulary

  • Wearables = smart electronic devices we wear
  • Wearable technology
  • Fashion statement = things you wear that show the type of person you are

Questions

  • Do you wear a watch?

    • I used to, but I don’t anymore. I don’t really see the need to, as my phone can tell me the time, as well as a zillion (a lot of) other things. I know wearables are coming back in fashion, but they’re not really for me.

  • Have you ever got a watch as a gift?
  • Why do some people wear expensive watches?

    • For those people, I guess an expensive watch is a fashion statement. It’s a way for them to show off their wealth or status. I suppose they may also think that the best quality watches are the most expensive ones.

  • Is it important to wear a watch?

Old buildings

Vocabulary

  • A listed building (U.K.) = protected by the government
  • In disrepair = in bad condition
  • In ruins = in very bad condition

Questions

  • Have you ever seen old buildings in a city?

    • Yes, I have, there are some old historic (or historical) buildings in my city which are listed buildings, and protected by the government. As well, there is also a handful of (a lot of / several) derelict buildings which to be honest, are falling apart and nearly in ruins.

  • Should we preserve old buildings in cities?

    • It depends really, if they have heritage or cultural value, then yes, on the other hand if they are in disrepair and potentially dangerous, then it would be better to knock them down.

  • Do you prefer to live in an old building or a modern one?
  • Are there any old buildings you want to visit in future?

Dreams

Vocabulary

  • A dream come true
  • A nightmare = a bad dream
  • A recurring dream = a dream you have repeatedly

Questions

  • Do you usually remember dreams you have had?

    • From time to time I do, especially if it’s one of those recurring dreams. Sometimes I remember a dream as soon as I wake up and it’s really vivid. Other times, I can only remember bits and pieces, maybe because the dream was just illogical and didn’t seem to make any sense.

  • Do you tell other people about your dreams?
  • Do you think dreams have a special meaning?

    • I am not sure, maybe. Sometimes I recall a dream and I see how it may represent a strong desire I have, like I often dream about flying and I suppose this symbolises a desire to be free and to do whatever I want.

    • No, not really, I think they’re just a chance for our imagination to go wild or an outlet for suppressed frustrations or desires. To be totally honest, I don’t go in for the idea that dreams have hidden, subconscious meanings.

  • Would you like for your dreams to come true?

Mirrors

Vocabulary

  • A reflection
  • To check my look = to check appearance
  • To be vain = to be self-loving

Questions

  • Do you like looking at yourself in the mirror?

    • Not particularly (not really), I mean I do check my reflection in the mornings to make sure my hair is tidy and my clothes are straight (neat and tidy) , but I don’t spend hours gazing at myself, brushing my hair or doing my make-up.

  • Have you ever bought a mirror?
  • Do you usually carry a mirror with you?

    • No, I don’t really need a mirror when I am out and about. If I need to check my look at work or when I’m out with friends, then I use a bathroom mirror. Of course nowadays, I can also use the camera on my phone, if needs be (if necessary).

    • Yes, I have a compact mirror that I take everywhere with me, in my handbag, just in case. You never know when I may need to touch up my make up (tidy up), or brush my hair.

  • Would you use mirrors to decorate your room?

Getting Lost

Vocabulary

  • To lose / find your way
  • A good / bad sense of direction
  • To be prone to getting lost = to often get lost
  • To point someone in the direction of

Questions

  • Have you ever got lost?

    • Absolutely. I mean (classify) I have a pretty good sense of direction, but I have got lost several times, especially in unfamiliar cities, as a tourist. I got lost in the back streets of Rome once, and had to ask for directions.

  • How can you find your way when you are lost?
  • Do you know how to read a map?

    • Yes, I would say so, but to be honest, nowadays with Google maps, I don’t think we need to ‘read’ maps. I can just set the directions and Google does the rest. It’s a piece of cake. I can get from one place to another on automatic pilot (without having to think).

  • Have you ever helped someone who was lost?
