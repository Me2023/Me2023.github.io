Part 1 - Strategies and skills

Common triggers or questions

The 6 most common triggers or questions in IELTS Speaking Part 1 are:

  • Do you VERB?
    • How long you have done it?

      • I’ve played since I was a child.

    • How often you do it?

      • regularly, once in a blue moon, every now and again, on a fairly regular basis

      • on a daily / weekly /monthly basis

      • rarely / hardly ever

  • Are you good at it?
    • Abilities

      • I have a bit of a knack for playing the guitar

      • I wouldn’t say I’m gifted but I can play fairly well

      • I can hold my own

      • I am pretty good at it

  • Did you VERB as a child?
    • Talk about past habits

      • I used to play when…

      • I would play in my free time

      • I often play at home

  • Do you prefer A or B?
    • Preference, compare and evaluate
    • Say which

      • I much prefer A to B, I tend to like A

    • Say why

      • A is far better than B because…

      • A is easier to use than B

  • Do you think NOUN is important?
    • Opinion, compare and evaluate
    • Give an opinion;

      • Absolutely, without a doubt, for sure

    • Say why;

      • We can learn so much from our family

    • Give an example;
  • Which NOUN is popular in your country?
    • Opinion, compare and evaluate
    • Give an opinion;

      • I reckon, I’d say, as far as I can see…

    • Say why;

      • want to be famous, make a lot of money

    • Give an example;

      • when I was in school, all of my friends wanted to be

Super strategies and top tips

  1. Short but expand
  2. Answer directly + why + example
  3. Talk about yourself
    1. Talk about more general ideas in Part 3!
  4. Be natural …with a new friend
  5. Know common question types

Learn to activate vocabulary

  1. Learn words in context
  2. Learn from sounds

  • Do you work or are you a student?

    • I work for a bank / a school / the government

    • I’ve worked there for fifteen years or so.

    • Basically my job involves designing products

      • Basically my job involves selling products
        Basically my job involves teaching others

  • What do you like about your work?

    • The best part about my job is the work environment.
      The best part about my job is my collegues.
      The best part about my job is the salary.

    • I also love the fact that we have flexitime
      I also love the fact that I am not on a 9 to 5 schedule
      I also love the fact that we get lots of training.

    • What’s more, I get to (can) work from home sometimes.
      What’s more, I get to meet some interesting people
      What’s more, I get to work with some great people

Part 1 - Model Answers

Accessories: Watches

Vocabulary

  • Wearables = smart electronic devices we wear
  • Wearable technology
  • Fashion statement = things you wear that show the type of person you are

Questions

  • Do you wear a watch?

    • I used to, but I don’t anymore. I don’t really see the need to, as my phone can tell me the time, as well as a zillion (a lot of) other things. I know wearables are coming back in fashion, but they’re not really for me.

  • Have you ever got a watch as a gift?
  • Why do some people wear expensive watches?

    • For those people, I guess an expensive watch is a fashion statement. It’s a way for them to show off their wealth or status. I suppose they may also think that the best quality watches are the most expensive ones.

  • Is it important to wear a watch?

Old buildings

Vocabulary

  • A listed building (U.K.) = protected by the government
  • In disrepair = in bad condition
  • In ruins = in very bad condition

Questions

  • Have you ever seen old buildings in a city?

    • Yes, I have, there are some old historic (or historical) buildings in my city which are listed buildings, and protected by the government. As well, there is also a handful of (a lot of / several) derelict buildings which to be honest, are falling apart and nearly in ruins.

  • Should we preserve old buildings in cities?

    • It depends really, if they have heritage or cultural value, then yes, on the other hand if they are in disrepair and potentially dangerous, then it would be better to knock them down.

  • Do you prefer to live in an old building or a modern one?
  • Are there any old buildings you want to visit in future?

Dreams

Vocabulary

  • A dream come true
  • A nightmare = a bad dream
  • A recurring dream = a dream you have repeatedly

Questions

  • Do you usually remember dreams you have had?

    • From time to time I do, especially if it’s one of those recurring dreams. Sometimes I remember a dream as soon as I wake up and it’s really vivid. Other times, I can only remember bits and pieces, maybe because the dream was just illogical and didn’t seem to make any sense.

  • Do you tell other people about your dreams?
  • Do you think dreams have a special meaning?

    • I am not sure, maybe. Sometimes I recall a dream and I see how it may represent a strong desire I have, like I often dream about flying and I suppose this symbolises a desire to be free and to do whatever I want.

    • No, not really, I think they’re just a chance for our imagination to go wild or an outlet for suppressed frustrations or desires. To be totally honest, I don’t go in for the idea that dreams have hidden, subconscious meanings.

  • Would you like for your dreams to come true?

Mirrors

Vocabulary

  • A reflection
  • To check my look = to check appearance
  • To be vain = to be self-loving

Questions

  • Do you like looking at yourself in the mirror?

    • Not particularly (not really), I mean I do check my reflection in the mornings to make sure my hair is tidy and my clothes are straight (neat and tidy) , but I don’t spend hours gazing at myself, brushing my hair or doing my make-up.

  • Have you ever bought a mirror?
  • Do you usually carry a mirror with you?

    • No, I don’t really need a mirror when I am out and about. If I need to check my look at work or when I’m out with friends, then I use a bathroom mirror. Of course nowadays, I can also use the camera on my phone, if needs be (if necessary).

    • Yes, I have a compact mirror that I take everywhere with me, in my handbag, just in case. You never know when I may need to touch up my make up (tidy up), or brush my hair.

  • Would you use mirrors to decorate your room?

Getting Lost

Vocabulary

  • To lose / find your way
  • A good / bad sense of direction
  • To be prone to getting lost = to often get lost
  • To point someone in the direction of

Questions

  • Have you ever got lost?

    • Absolutely. I mean (classify) I have a pretty good sense of direction, but I have got lost several times, especially in unfamiliar cities, as a tourist. I got lost in the back streets of Rome once, and had to ask for directions.

  • How can you find your way when you are lost?
  • Do you know how to read a map?

    • Yes, I would say so, but to be honest, nowadays with Google maps, I don’t think we need to ‘read’ maps. I can just set the directions and Google does the rest. It’s a piece of cake. I can get from one place to another on automatic pilot (without having to think).

  • Have you ever helped someone who was lost?

Wallets

Questions

  • Do you usually carry a wallet?

    • Yes, I certainly do, I don’t think I could get by without one. I have a brown leather one, I’ve had it for donkey’s years actually, so it’s a bit tatty and frayed and I use it to carry my credit cards, ID, a family photo and the like (and things like that).

  • Have you ever lost your wallet?

    • Yes, and it was a nightmare. I had to cancel my bank cards, and reapply for a new ID. It was also a shame that I lost a couple of photos that have sentimental value for me. Luckily, I hardly had any cash in it.

    • No, I haven’t but I my brother did and it was a nightmare. He had to…

    • No, but I did have one stolen once when I was on a train. It was a nightmare because I had to….

    • No, I haven’t, but if I did lose one, I suppose I would be really annoyed, because it would be a nightmare, I’d have to…

  • Have you ever sent a wallet to someone as a gift?
  • Do most of your friends use wallets?

The Sky and Stars

Vocabulary

  • A constellation = a pattern of stars
  • The night sky
  • A sunrise at dawn / a sunset at dusk
  • The horizon = skyline

Questions

  • Do you like to look at the sky?

    • on purpose

    • Yes, for sure. Whenever I get the chance, I love to take in a beautiful sunrise or sunset. Mind you, (but) I do live in a city, so the sky is often overcast (dull and cloudy) or polluted and it’s not so easy to catch sight of the sun or the stars.

  • Do you know much about the stars?

    • To be honest, not a great deal. I mean, I learnt about the the milky way at school, and actually I have picked up (learn informally) the names of a few common constellations from a mobile app. So, I learnt about the Great Bear and Orion, things like that.

  • How did you learn about the stars?

Flowers

Roses, Tulips

Jasmine, Carnations, Sunflowers

Vocabulary

  • A bunch of flowers
  • To water a flower
  • Petals
  • To have green fingers

Questions

  • What kinds of flowers do you like?

    • I am not really a flower person, but I do buy flowers for our home now and again (sometimes, from time to time), and I usually go for (choose) carnations or roses. I find the bright colours and soft petals can give a fresh and relaxed feeling to the room.

  • Have you planted any flowers?
  • Have you ever sent flowers to anyone?
  • Are there any flowers that have special meanings in your country?

    • That’s a good question, I think the most wellknown one is the rose. A rose usually symbolises (represents, expresses) love and passion, so (if we wanted) we would give a red rose to someone to express our love for that person, or you might give it to someone you want to go out with (go on a date with).

Advertisements

Adverts, advertising

Useful collocations

  • An advertising campaign
  • A captivating / engaging (good) advert
  • An intrusive advert
  • An engaging slogan

Questions

  • Do you see a lot of advertising?

    • For sure, I think nowadays we are bombarded with (be hit again and again and again) adverts left, right and centre. I see them on my social media feeds, on website pop ups, and to be honest, by and large (generally speaking, most of time) I find them quite intrusive.

  • Are there any types of advertisements that you like?

    • Well, yes, some adverts that tell a story can be quite engaging. So rather than focusing on the product features, they focus on the user and their emotions. I think these are easier to relate to and that emotional connection is really powerful.

  • Have you ever bought something because of an advertisement?
  • Do you watch advertisements from the beginning to the end?

Farms and Farming

Vocabulary

  • Livestock
    • Sheep, cows, horses
  • To milk a cow = to get milk from a cow
  • To raise / breed chickens = to feed and look after

Questions

  • Have you ever visited a farm before?

    • Yes, I have, but to be honest it was a long time ago, when I was at school. We had a school trip to a farm where they rear (breed, raise) livestock, mainly cows and sheep, and we had a chance to feed them. Actually, it was quite an eye-opener for me, as it was my first time on a farm.

  • Did you do farm work when you were young?
  • Would you like to live on a farm?
  • Do you think farming is important?

    • Absolutely, I reckon it’s the backbone of our economy. If we didn’t have farms, we wouldn’t have any food and we would starve, I guess. To my understanding, it’s also a key source of (an important source of) employment in many countries.

      Chicken is a key source of protein
      Milk is a key source of calcium

Singing

The arts (humanities):

Painting, Music, Dance

Questions

  • Do you like singing?

    • Well, I enjoy listening to others sing, but me personally, I think I’m tone deaf (can’t hear different musical notes). As much as I might try (Even though I try a lot), I just can’t hit the right notes; I am totally out of tune (sing the wrong notes), every time, without fail (every time).

      As much as I try, I just can’t get up in the morning
      As much as I try, I just can’t do this homework
      As much as I try, I just can’t speak English

    • Yes, I love singing, you know, when I am pottering about (doing small jobs, simple activities in a relaxed way) at home, doing the housework or even in the shower, but that said, I have never sung in front of an audience. (If some one asked me,) I wouldn’t have the stomach (be brave enough) to do that.

  • Did you enjoy singing when you were a child?
  • Will you take singing lessons in the future?
  • Would you like to be a singer?

Handwriting

Vocabulary

  • Typing with/on a keyboard
  • Typing on a computer
  • Writing with a pen
  • Calligraphy = the art of writing
  • Graphology = the study of handwriting (and what it reveals / show about you)

Questions

  • Do you often write with a pen?
  • Can you tell someone’s personality from his or her handwriting?
  • Do you think people will still write letters in the future?

    • I’d say so yes, I don’t think we are about to give up writing letters completely.
      To my mind, many people feel they can express their emotions better in a letter, and handwriting is more personal, so I think we will still be writing letters in the years to come (in the upcoming years, in the future).

  • What do you think are the differences between writing with a pen and typing on a computer?

    • Well, the way I see it, writing with a pen is more expressive, because everyone has their own style of handwriting which apparently can reveal a lot about themselves. That said, typing is much neater (far neater, way neater) and it’s easier to edit, you don’t need to rub things out (erase).

Sleep

  • How many hours do you sleep every day (or every night)?
  • Do you think it’s good to have a nap during the day?
    • at noon

    • Yes, I do think it’s a good idea to have a small sleep during the day, but if you sleep too long, you can end up feeling groggy and tired for the rest of the day!

    • Yes, it is a good idea to have a nap, otherwise you are going to feel really tired for the rest of the day.

  • Did you sleep more in the past than you do now?

Sharing

  • Do you enjoy sharing?
  • What do you share with your friends?
  • Were you encouraged to share as a child?

    • Yes, both my mother and father encouraged me to share my toys and even my food with friends, because they felt that was a family value I should grow up with.

    • Yes, I’d say I was strongly encouraged to share with family and friends, if not I would get into big trouble with my parents.

Maths

  • When did you start to learn maths?
  • Is maths important?

    • Yes, I think it is important in today’s society, for instance, when you go shopping, if you want the right change, you need to know addition and subtraction.

    • I think maths is quite important nowadays, that said, today everybody has a mobile phone so I am not sure we need to know addition and subtraction, we can just use a calculator on our phones.

  • What is a good way to learn maths?

Bottled Water

  • Do you ever drink bottled water? Why?

    • Yes, I do, it’s healthier than tap water so, I usually buy bottled water at the supermarket.

    • No, I don’t usually drink bottled water because it’s the same as tap water so, I don’t bother to buy it at the supermarket.

    • I guess I do sometimes drink bottled water like in a restaurant, mind you (however, at the same time), at home I usually drink tap water because I trust it and it’s the same really!

  • Do you think it is important to drink a lot of water?
  • Is water an important resource?

Rubbish

  • Is it important to keep the streets clean?
  • Why do some people throw rubbish on the ground?

    • It depends really, I think if someone lives in the city, maybe they’re just lazy, if some people have grown up in the countryside, maybe it’s just that they don’t know it can damage the environment.

    • I’d say some people just don’t care, they think just one person doesn’t make a difference. At the same time, some people, especially younger children, may not know it can damage the environment.

  • What do you do when you see rubbish on the street?

Perfume

  • Do you wear perfume?
  • What’s your favourite perfume?
  • Would you spend a lot of money on a perfume?
    • Yes I think I would. If I was buying a present for somebody, I’d be happy to splash out on a nice perfume.
    • It depends really. If I was buying it for myself, I don’t think I would spend too much, if I was buying a present for somebody, then yes, I’d fork out a bit of extra money.

Shopping

  • Do you like shopping?

    • Well, I like shopping for food (food shopping), but I am not a big fan of clothes shopping. I love cooking, so I really enjoy picking up (buying) fresh produce (fruits or vegetables) at the local markets, but when it comes to buying clothes, I try to get it over and done with (get it quickly done), as quickly as I can.

  • Who does most of the shopping in your household?
  • What is the item that you buy the most?
  • Do you compare prices before purchasing something?

    • I think for food items, yes I probably do. I am usually after the best value for money (best quality and price), so I will (as a habit) compare brands and prices before purchasing. It’s become a bit of (softer) a habit really.

Staying up late

  • Do you sometimes go to bed late?

    • Now and again yes, but by and large (generally speaking, overall) I tend to hit the sack (go to bed) quite early. I am usually an early riser, because I find I am much more productive in the mornings.

    • Yes, actually, I tend to stay up until the early hours. I just enjoy the peace and quiet when others have gone to bed. It’s a great time to get things done.

  • What do you do when you stay up late?
  • How do you feel when you have stayed up late the night before?

    • Well, if I have stayed up late and need to work the next day, I will usually feel a bit tired and groggy (weak / sick from lack of sleep / water).
      You know, if I don’t get at least 7 hours sleep, I usually feel out of sorts (don’t feel well, fell under the weather).

    • To be honest, mostly, I’m fine because I am used to it. So long as I get around 6 hours sleep, I don’t feel too tired or sleepy. I just get myself a strong coffee first thing in the morning and I am good to go (am ready to begin).

Tidiness

  • Are you a tidy person?

    • Yes, quite tidy. I make a habit of (positive) making my bed in the morning, I usually wash the pots straight after dinner. I wouldn’t say my house is spic and span (very clean and tidy), but it’s tidy enough.

    • Yes, I am a stickler for (strict about) keeping things tidy. I like my home to be really shipshape. If the house is messy, it just puts me in a bad mood, you know, I can’t find things and I also feel bad because I know I should’ve cleaned up, but I haven’t.

  • Are all members of your family tidy?
  • Do you think people should be tidy all the time?
  • Are you more tidy at work/school or at home?

    • Interestingly enough, I tend to keep my workplace in order, way more (much more) than my home. I guess I just think tidiness and productivity are connected, but at home I just want a more relaxed atmosphere.

Breaks

  • Do you prefer a long break or several short breaks?

    • By and large (overall, generally speaking)， I prefer several short breaks. Mainly because I have a bit of a short attention span, but also I think it’s much more productive to have more frequent breaks. That said, I do like to have a long lunch break!

  • How often do you take a break?
  • What do you usually do during a break?

    • Sometimes, I’ll (‘wiil’ can be used for present habits) pop out (go out) for a quick coffee so I can stretch my legs as well, and other times, I might go and chat to some colleagues. A bunch of them usually go for a (walk, sport) smoke outside, so I might join them, even though I am not a smoker.

  • Why do you need to take a break?

Laughter

  • When was the last time you laughed?

    • That’s hard to say, I think it was probably last week. I was watching a film and it really made me giggle. It was a comedy with Jim Carey and if I remember correctly it was called ‘Yes Man’. It was one of those films that’s full of visual humour, right? and it really cracked me up (made me laugh a lot).

  • Do you like watching funny programmes on TV?
  • Do you like to make people laugh?

    • I do, I think I have a good sense of humour, I enjoy a good laugh with friends. I am not really into telling jokes (I don’t like, I’m not a big fan of), but I like playing with words, so in conversations I am often thinking about other meanings words can have, and then make a joke with that.

  • Do you think it is important for friends to laugh together?

History

  • Do you like history?

    • I wouldn’t say I am a big fan of history, but I do enjoy watching historical (related to history; historic: famous in history) films or period dramas (show / film set in the past) on TV. I find it fascinating to see the costumes and lifestyle of people in past times (in the past). For example, I used to watch Downtown Abbey and loved seeing how different society was then.

  • Did you enjoy learning history when you were a child?

    • To be honest, not much. It tended to (it used to be, it usually was) be all facts and figures that I couldn’t remember very well. So it was a bit tedious (boring, monotonous). It was only when I left school and started travelling that I started to get a taste for (to begin to like) history.

  • When was the last time you read about history?
  • Do you think history is important?

Wild Animals

Wild animals = not tame, not domesticated

  • Have you ever seen wild animals before?

    • Well, I have seen lots in the zoo, such as lions and elephants, but I’ve never seen those kinds of animals in the wild. When I was a kid growing up, I saw lots of foxes, because there were plenty of them in the forests near where I used to live.

  • Which wild animals do you like the most?
  • Do you like to go to the zoo?

    • Yes and no. I guess on the one hand, zoos are fun and it’s amazing to get up close (to be really close) to those magnificent creatures like the lions. But, on the other hand, the cages in many zoos are too cramped (not enough space) and the living conditions of many animals leave a lot to be desired (not good enough).

Wearing Jeans

  • Do you like wearing jeans? Why?

    • For sure, I wear jeans an awful lot because they are just so comfy (comfortable) and practical. That said, I don’t wear them in summer so much, because it’s usually too hot. I think they are more suitable for the cooler seasons.

  • Why are they so popular?
  • Do most people like to wear jeans?
  • Do you prefer to wear a certain brand of jeans?
  • Why do people in your country wear jeans?

    • I’d say in large part (mainly, largely), because they have been made so fashionable by celebrities and the big clothes brands. I mean take Calvin Klein for example they have created an image of a young person with jeans as being cool and sexy, an image they many young people strive to emulate (try to imitate, follow, be like).

Hairstyles

a fringe, short back and sides

  • Have you always had the hairstyle you have now?

    • No, not at all. I mean I wear my hair really short now, because it’s much more manageable (easy to manage or handle), you know, easy to wash, no need to brush it and that. But when I was younger I used to have long, shoulder-length hair. I also played with the idea of dying it, but in the end, I never did.

  • Do you go to the barber’s a lot?
    • the barber’s = the hair dresser’s = the hair salon = the place you get your hair cut

    • To tell you the truth, I never go to the hairdressers’s these days, because my wife cuts my hair. However, when I was younger I used to go once a month, and I always got the same style; short back and sides.

  • Did you ever feel disappointed with your hairstyle?

Voice

  • How would you describe your voice?

    • That’s a tough question, I guess I would describe it as quite low-pitched (high-pitched) and slightly husky (smooth, husky = rough = hoarse). Some people say I have a bit of a radio voice, you know, they say it’s quite clear. Mind you, I think I am quite soft-spoken (loud voice, soft-spoken voice), so maybe it’s not the best voice for the radio.

  • Did you have the same voice as a child?
  • Do you like your voice?
  • Do you ever record your voice?

    • It’s funny you should ask (What a coincidence, coincidentally), because actually I just started recording my voice recently. My Chinese teacher recommended it as a way to improve my speaking skills, so I record some phrases with a mobile phone app, and then compare my pronunciation with a native speaker recording.

Concentration

  • What helps you concentrate?

    • Peace and quiet. If there’s lots of background noise going on, even if it’s not really loud, then it distracts (disturb, interrupt) me and I just can’t focus on whatever I am doing. So when I am reading or studying for example, I’m a stickler for (insist on) silence.

  • What stops you from concentrating?
  • What do you do when you can’t concentrate?

    • I find the best thing to do is stop whatever I am doing and go and do something else. I mean I have some techniques I try, like breaking my work down into 20 minute chunks, or drinking water, and that helps. But, more often than not (usually), I just change activity.
